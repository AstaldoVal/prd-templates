Agile PRD template

Context
The goal of agile development is to deliver value quickly while staying closely aligned with customer needs. Cross-functional teams work incrementally — refining requirements and priorities as they learn.

This agile PRD template is a streamlined version of the traditional full document. It is designed to capture just what the development team needs to build and iterate effectively.

---
Structure

1. Objective
   - Description of what you want to build
   - Include how this effort aligns to overall strategy

2. Background and assumptions
   - Background information for the team (personas, customer interviews, etc.)
   - Assumptions behind your thinking

3. Features / user stories
   - List of planned features or user stories that will support the objective

4. User experience (flow and design)
   - Link to UX artifacts: user story map, wireframes, or other design explorations

5. Constraints and dependencies
   - Roadblocks the team has identified
   - Timing of other work
   - Impacted functionality

6. Open questions
   - Unknowns the team may run into but has not yet answered
