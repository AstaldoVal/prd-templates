Peter Yang's PRD Template – 7 Sections

Principles: Short and to the point; living document; seven key sections

1. Header
   - Links to designs and other artifacts
   - Team names
   - Last updated date

2. Problem
   - Who is the customer?
   - What is the customer problem?
   - How do we know this is a problem?
   (Few sentences)

3. Goals
   - One output goal for the feature
   - 2-3 input metrics to drive output
   - Non-goals and guardrail metrics when relevant

4. Solution
   - High-level solution and key milestones
   - Per milestone: user stories, P0/P1 marking, open topics for async discussion
   - Wireframes/design optional

5. Launch plan
   - Staged rollout or experiment
   - For experiments: success criteria, eligibility, test, control, ramp plan

6. Meeting Notes
   - Take meeting notes directly in PRD
   - Capture latest discussions and decisions in single document

7. Appendix
   - Keep PRD short
   - Link to: customer interviews, competitive research, supporting data

---
AI workflow (Peter Yang):
1. Draft bullets (problem, goal, solution)
2. Discuss async with stakeholders and customers
3. Use AI to fill template from raw chat logs
4. Result: PRD with customer/stakeholder validation, no meetings
